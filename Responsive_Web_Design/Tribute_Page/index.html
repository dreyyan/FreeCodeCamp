Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace (1815–1852) was a pioneering mathematician and writer who is widely considered to be the first computer programmer. She wrote the first algorithm intended to be processed by a machine — the Analytical Engine designed by Charles Babbage. Her foresight into the possibilities of computing, far beyond simple number-crunching, laid the groundwork for modern computer science.
